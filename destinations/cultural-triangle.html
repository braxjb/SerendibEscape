Why visit

The ancient heart of Sri Lanka

The Cultural Triangle connects some of Sri Lanka’s most important historic sites, including Sigiriya, Dambulla, Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa. It is ideal for travellers who want culture, photography, soft adventure and meaningful guided experiences.

The region works beautifully at the beginning of a Sri Lanka itinerary before travelling south to Kandy, the tea country, wildlife parks or the coast.

Where to stay

Boutique bases near Sigiriya and Dambulla

The best stays in this region are usually peaceful boutique hotels, nature lodges and villas surrounded by paddy fields, lakes and jungle. This gives travellers a calm base after warm days exploring temples and ruins.

Good to know

FAQs about the Cultural Triangle

How many nights should I spend in the Cultural Triangle?

Two to four nights is ideal depending on how deeply you want to explore Sigiriya, Dambulla, Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa.

Is Sigiriya difficult to climb?

The climb has many steps and can feel tiring in warm weather. Starting early in the morning makes it more comfortable.

Can I combine the Cultural Triangle with a safari?

Yes. Minneriya, Kaudulla and Eco Park are popular options for elephant safaris depending on the season.
